The Greek Alphabet

The Greek alphabet was first introduced around 3000 BC. The modern Greek alphabet is theorised to have been introduced around 1453; the fall of the Byzantine Empire. The only difference between the Ancient Greek and Modern Greek alphabet is the pronounciation of letters, so let's dive into it!

The Ancient Greek Alphabet

  1. Α α - Alpha

  2. Β β - Beta

  3. Γ γ - Gamma

  4. Δ δ - Delta

  5. Ε ε - Epsilon

  6. Ζ ζ - Zeta

  7. Η η - Eta

  8. Θ θ - Theta

  9. Ι ι - Iota

  10. Κ κ - Kappa

  11. Λ λ - Lambda

  12. Μ μ - Mu

  13. Ν ν - Nu

  14. Ξ ξ - Xi

  15. Ο ο - Omikron

  16. Π π - Pi

  17. Ρ ρ - Rho

  18. Σ σ ς - Sigma

  19. Τ τ - Tau

  20. Υ υ - Ypsilon

  21. Φ φ - Phi

  22. Χ χ -Chi

  23. Ψ ψ - Psi

  24. Ω ω - Omega

Fun fact: Did you know that the word "Alphabet" comes from the first two Ancient Greek letters "Alpha" & "Beta" ?
