Articles in greek

Intro

In greek we have a LOT of articles and 4 cases: Nominative, accusative, genitive and vocative. We'll begin with nomiνative, you use nominative when something or someone is the subject of a sentence. There are definitive and indefinitive articles in greek with 3 genders: Masculine, feminine & neuter.

Masculine, feminine & neuter nominative articles

Let's begin, the definite article in english is 'the' and indefinite: 'a' or 'an'. The masculine def. nom. article in greek is 'Ο' in lowercase it's: 'ο', the feminine one is: 'Η' in lowercase: 'η' and for neuter: 'Το' in lowercase: 'το'.

How to know a gender of the word

You might think that you would have to master and remember the genders of greek words but luckily for you, you can actually find the gender of a word depending on the end of it. Still don't understand? Let me show you!

Masculine case

Most of the time when a word ends in -ας, ης, ος it's masculine singular
